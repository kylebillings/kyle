Launching Intensives

Codecademy had developed a new paid offering but hadn’t established key components of the enrollment funnel. I spearheaded the project as my first with the company.

Buyer Profiles

I compiled buyer profiles to root each constituent level of the funnel in an understanding of a typical buyer’s learning style, career goals, and other ethnographic vectors. This was an iterative process, and we continued to test these profiles against subsequent cohorts of learners.

Messaging

With the launch of the first Intensive program, Codecademy faced a unique challenge — how might we position a $500, 8-week program alongside our free product and a $19.99/month subscription with similar features?

I asked my team to imagine an auditorium with Codecademy on stage. We turn nobody away. Everyone gets the information. But the front row gets the details, the eye contact, and the feeling of proximity. Their questions are heard loudly and clearly.

When we speak to Intensive learners, we recognize their career goals and tech adjacency. We take it seriously. We respect their time constraints and get to the point. But we’re still the witty teacher that makes you laugh in class.

Email

Email was both a product requirement and venue for experimentation.

The operations team and I standardized the tone of course-related emails and built a template in customer.io.

A marketing contractor and I tested upsell emails with different segments of our user base throughout the buying cycle. He managed the email sends and collected results, and I incorporated our findings into product work.

Landing Page

On the landing page, I illustrated how the unique depth of the program, with a heightened feeling of accountability through one-on-one mentorship, brought career aspirations into arms reach for a fraction the investment of in-person bootcamps.

Using a platform called Unbounce to free up engineering time, I A/B tested this proposition, along with variations in length, structure, and imagery, to strike the right balance between lead quality and CTR for the best performance down the funnel.

Payments

Codecademy’s payment system was set up for subscriptions and incompatible with the new offering. I explored edge cases and provided compatible designs for payment, cancellation, refund, and receipt experiences.

Results

The messaging strategy, emails, landing page, and payments experience were foundational for the launch and have been utilized for every subsequent program release.

Repeatable Revenue

Codecademy was exhausting its email list and needed a sustainable source of enrollments to its Intensive programs.

Growth

I managed a dedicated growth team to help pursue Codecademy’s quarterly targets. I studied existing metrics, diagramed conversion rates across the site to identify high-impact opportunities for growth, and produced a roadmap of growth projects.
