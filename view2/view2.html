I am lucky enough to work with a team of brilliant scientists on this project. After a simple cheek-swab test, our algorithm can create a thoroughly personalized nutrition plan based on the particular way one's DNA creates and regulates proteins. In other words, we can recommend foods in an 'achieve x by eating y' format.

Bison happens to make my body produce muscle quicker than other protein.

As lead designer for the company, my job is to deliver a hopeful, supportive interface through which users can interact with the foods that may help them to achieve better well-being.



Iteration 1: Data Oriented

Built for the biohackers

The first iteration of the product listed the top 5 foods in each of seven food groups and focused on the architecture of traceback data delivered by the backend.

Each recommendation expanded to reveal the genetics behind each decision.

(Sweet Potatoes > Facilitative Glucose Transport > AMY1 gene)

Iteration 2: Lifestyle Change (current)

Help users take action

Whereas the first iteration tilted towards science, the second is accessible and actionable. I've switched over to XCode for development while we take in feedback from our beta users.
