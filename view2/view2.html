I am lucky enough to work with a team of brilliant scientists on this project. After a simple cheek-swab test, our algorithm can create a thoroughly personalized nutrition plan based on the particular way one's DNA creates and regulates proteins. In other words, we can recommend foods in an 'achieve x by eating y' format.

Bison happens to make my body produce muscle quicker than other protein.

As lead designer for the company, my job is to deliver a hopeful, supportive interface through which users can interact with the foods that may help them to achieve better well-being.



Iteration 1: Data Oriented

Built for the biohackers


The first alpha-iteration of the product listed the top 5 foods in each of seven food groups and focused on the architecture of traceback data delivered by the backend.

Each recommendation expanded to reveal the genetics behind each decision. I quickly designed a layout that kept the list skimmable, but simply allowed for more granular study.


For each recommendation, users could uncover several additional layers of detail.



Checkout Process

I had a lot of fun creating the DNA-test kit/DNA Analysis checkout process. All users followed a series of steps that split into different buckets depending on the particular order; every step dynamically added the next fields onto the form, which kept information in context and felt comfortable.



Iteration 2: Lifestyle Change (current)
