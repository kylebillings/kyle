I was lucky enough to work with a team of brilliant scientists on this project. After a simple cheek-swab test, our algorithm created a thoroughly personalized nutrition plan based on the particular way one's DNA creates and regulates proteins. In other words, it recommended foods in an 'achieve x by eating y' format.

For example, bison happens to make my body produce muscle quicker than other protein.

As lead designer for the company, my goals were to deliver a hopeful, supportive interface through which users interacted with the information that may help them to achieve better well-being.


Iteration 1: Data Oriented

Built for the biohackers


The first alpha-iteration of the product listed the top 5 foods in each of seven food groups and focused on the architecture of traceback data delivered by the backend.

Each recommendation expanded to reveal the genetics behind each decision. I quickly designed a layout that kept the list skimmable, but allowed for more granular study.

Click here to view the prototype I created.



Checkout Process

I had a lot of fun creating the DNA-test kit/DNA Analysis checkout process. All users followed a series of steps that split into different buckets depending on the particular order; every step dynamically added the next fields onto the form, which kept information in context and felt comfortable.
