Launching Intensives

Codecademy had developed a new 8-week online intensive but hadn’t established key components of the purchase funnel. I worked with different teams to build each level of a cohesive buying experience that could be used for all future releases.

Research

Through interviews and quantitative analysis, I compiled buyer profiles to highlight a typical buyer’s learning style, career goals, and other ethnographic vectors. We tested these profiles against beta cohorts and used them to inform the product experience and messaging strategy that tied the funnel together.

Messaging

With the launch of the first Intensive program (then called Codecademy Ready), the company faced a unique challenge — how might we position a $500, 8-week program alongside our free product and a $19.99/month subscription with similar features?

I wrote an initial set of guidelines in which we recognize a leaner's career goals and tech adjacency. We recognized a need to stay relevant. We take it seriously. We respect their time constraints and get to the point. But we’re still a witty teacher with a fixie by the door.

Email

Email was both a product requirement and venue for experimentation. The operations team and I created a template to standardize course-related emails and triggered sends in customer.io. With those in order, a marketing contractor and I tested upsell emails with different segments of our user base throughout the buying cycle. He managed the email sends and collected results, and I incorporated our findings into marketing and product work.

Landing Page

With the copy and design of the landing page, I illustrated how the unique depth of the program, with a heightened feeling of accountability through one-on-one mentorship, brought career aspirations into arms reach for a fraction the investment of in-person bootcamps.

Using a platform called Unbounce to free up engineering time, I A/B tested this proposition, along with variations in length, structure, and imagery, to strike the right balance between lead quality and CTR for the best performance down the funnel.

Payments

Codecademy’s payment system was set up for subscriptions and incompatible with the new offering. I explored edge cases and provided compatible designs for payment, cancellation, refund, and receipt experiences.

Results

The messaging strategy, emails, landing page, and payments experience were foundational for the launch and have been utilized for every subsequent program release.

Repeatable Revenue

Codecademy was exhausting its email list and needed a sustainable source of enrollments to its Intensive programs.

Expanding the Problem Space

Our team set out to create an on-platform experience that surfaced Intensives to new signups without cannibalizing revenue from Pro subscriptions.

I recorded dozens of users interacting with the site to present alongside four distinct personas, and pushed for us to look beyond ad units and consider learner needs before our own. I proposed that the same real-world context that Intensive learners use to make a purchase decision could help uninformed beginners choose a free course that excites them.

Catalog

With a nearing deadline and low engineering bandwidth, our team scoped the project to the essentials. The result was a re-categorization of the catalog into programming disciplines with descriptions, salary projections, and imagery. A short onboarding experience rearranged the sections and presented Intensives to learners with an interest in building websites.

Results

I provided ongoing creative direction for newsletters, course launches, partnerships, paid ads, and video productions. My foundational work has helped the now 6-person marketing team generate millions of dollars in revenue.
