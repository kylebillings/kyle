Redesigning Gone

User Research | UI/UX Design | Web | Team: Founders, Growth, Engineering

Gone is a full-service platform that helps people sell their used items by handling pricing, pickup, inspection, listing, and payment processing for users across the United States.

I performed a full redesign of Gone's iOS interface and marketing site to improve user experiences, create a trustworthy brand, and support the growth team's objectives. This was a great project since there are so many reasons people might sell so many different things.

I worked closely with the co-founders, marketers, and engineering teams to implement the resulting visual language across the company.

The Gone App

Wireframes | Sketch/After Effects Mockups | Titanium SDK | Team: CEO, CTO, iOS Dev

The Gone app involves real-world services and experiences easily change based on a user's items, his or her location, and buyers on the secondary market. The objective of our first redesign was to update the existing UI with an interface that helped users complete their unique flow without frustration or confusion. Our second redesign considers the varied motivations of our users and builds on the overlaps between declutterers, upgraders, and money-makers.

Stage one of the redesign, Gone 1.8, rebranded the app and clarified several screens and digital-to-physical hand-offs within the workflow. (For example, when a courier is scheduled to arrive at your home to pick up your items.)

Stage two of the redesign, Gone 2.0, responds to new user data and business objectives with an entirely reconsidered interface and user experience. For example, one target at this stage is fewer back-and-forth questions before an item is priced, which we are solving by populating item-specific questions with data gathered by Gone 1.8.

After Gone 1.8 launched, we noticed a substantial month-to-month increase in average item value, repeat users, and sign ups.

Gone SMS

User Research | UX Design | "Invisible" UI | Web | Team: Engineering, Support

Gone SMS lets users complete the entire Gone selling process via a text message conversation with our expert selling team.

The new platform not only made Gone available to users without iPhones, it provided a solution to the App's most troublesome UX issues — lack of communication and trust. Gone SMS places a solution to these challenges — a conversational interface — at the core of a service that anyone with a texting plan can access.

I designed mockups, wrote site copy, created templates and images for social media & advertisements, and wrote a large directory of situational responses for the operators.

Landing Page, Waitlist.

GoneSMS’s design is a derivative of the original branding of Gone. The site continues to suggest to users the outcome of a minimalist and deliberate lifestyle. I implemented the design and copy of the landing page.

GoneSMS was launched with a wait list and referral program. I worked with my colleagues to link up the front end with a custom dashboard to manage signups, referrals, wait list positions, rewards, and item sales.

My dashboard prototype can be found on Github.




Outcome

Both operators and users love the new conversational UX. Connecting the support team with users via text directly increases the number of sales per user and encourages the sale of higher value used goods. We found that Gone's overall average item value increased over 2.2x one month after the SMS launch.

Most interestingly, Gone SMS serves the company’s upcoming milestones by providing an opportunity for hands-on user research and insight into the expectations of younger sellers.

Actionable Genetics

User Research | UX Design | "Invisible" UI | Web (Rails)

I've worked with the founders of Within at several different stages in their development as founders of a quantitative health startup in Cambridge, Massachusetts and San Francisco. I've worked with a number of talented scientists and engineers to build exciting consumer-facing DNA, Blood, and Activity analysis software.

As a designer and front-end development, my goals have consistently been to deliver a hopeful, supportive interface through which users interact with information that may help them to achieve better well-being.

The first iteration of (a company I shall not name) was built on technology that parsed user genomes to generate a list of foods that best contribute to certain lifestyle goals: weight loss, muscle gain, and general well-being.

For example, according to science, bison encourages my body to produce muscle quicker than any other protein.

The product listed the top 5 foods in each of seven food groups. Each recommendation expanded to reveal the genetic markers and studies behind each decision. I designed a layout that kept the list skimmable, but allowed for more granular study.

My prototype is still available here.

Mobile Web

The second MVP of this product — a mobile web application — deemphasized genetics in exchange for greater emphasis on foods and good nutrition. While maintaining the benefit of genetically informed advice, this iteration took a step back from the heavy handed "eat this" approach that raised flags with several users.

While producing this more lenient product, we accepted that good health is best approached from all angles and began to consider the other avenues through which genetic analysis could more deeply affect our users.

This company disbanded for a time following this pivot. But don't worry — the story doesn't end there.
