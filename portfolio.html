Redesigning Gone

Gone is a very lean, interconnected system of selling services including pricing, in-home pickup, warehousing, listing, shipping, and payment processing that helps users cash in on unused items around their home. I performed a redesign with Gone's stakeholders to give context to the value proposition and improve several KPIs.

I worked closely with the co-founders, marketing, and engineering teams to create an actionable framework for communication that draws from the proven expertise of the company, the aspirations of its users and their expectations of the service.

Marketing Site

I mocked up and implemented the entire front end of the marketing site at thegoneapp.com with back end support from my colleagues in Argentina.

I graduated the logo and video from the original Gone site and introduced visual elements that communicate the sophisticated, deliberate, and human characteristics we hoped to affiliate with Gone. My objective was to use a light-hearted and calculated design language to combat any heavy feelings a user may have of clutter or of buyer's remorse.

The Gone App

Our redesign of Gone's brand included a new UI for the iOS application. I worked with the iOS engineers to apply beneficial interface changes to the app's existing functionality. In addition to updated colors, more informative icons, and smarter copy, the new version increased pricing transparency, emphasized in-app support, and facilitated the item upload process.

The new design meaningfully improved Gone's trustworthiness, and contributed to an increase in average item value, repeat users, and sign ups per month.

Gone SMS

Gone’s original app is an on-demand selling platform that facilitates the selling process from start to finish — identifying items for sale, pricing, packing, shipping, repairing, listing, and payment processing. GoneSMS is an alternate approach that places a solution to the original app’s biggest challenges — communication, updates, and transparency — at the very core of the service.

I designed mockups, wrote site copy, created templates and images for social media & advertisements, and wrote a large directory of situational responses for the operators.

Landing Page, Waitlist.

GoneSMS’s design is a derivative of the original branding of Gone. The site continues to suggest to users the outcome of a minimalist and deliberate lifestyle with Gone. I implemented the design and copy of the landing page in html, css, and js.

GoneSMS was launched with a wait list and referral program. I worked with my colleagues to link up the front end with a custom dashboard to manage signups, referrals, wait list positions, rewards, and item sales.

My dashboard prototype can be found on Github.

Outcome

Both operators and users love the new conversational UX. Connecting the support team with users via text directly increases the number of sales per user and encourages the sale of higher value used goods. Gone's overall average item value increased over 2.2x one month after the SMS launch.

GoneSMS not only activates the non-iOS userbase, it serves the company’s forthcoming milestones by providing an opportunity for hands-on user research and insight into the expectations of sellers.
